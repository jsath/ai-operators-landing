Is OpenClaw Worth It?
ROI Breakdown

You're a business person. You want numbers. Here they are. No fluff. Just the math on whether OpenClaw makes financial sense for your business.

The short answer: for most business owners spending $2K+ per month on VAs or manual labor, OpenClaw pays for itself in the first month. Often the first week.

But let me show the math so you can judge for yourself.

The cost side: what you'll spend

One-time setup

  • DIY: $0 in cash, 20-40 hours of your time
  • With an OpenClaw consultant: $1,000-3,000 depending on complexity

Let's use $1,500 as a typical setup with a consultant. That's the number for most standard business setups.

Monthly ongoing

  • Server (VPS): $20-40/month
  • AI API costs: $50-150/month depending on usage
  • Total monthly: $70-190/month

Let's use $150/month as a reasonable estimate for a business running multiple workflows.

The value side: what you'll save

Scenario 1: Replacing a VA

If you currently have a VA doing repetitive work (inbox, CRM, scheduling, research, follow-ups):

  • VA cost: $2,000-5,000/month
  • OpenClaw cost: ~$150/month (after setup)
  • Monthly savings: $1,850-4,850/month
  • Year 1 savings: $18,700-54,300 (minus $1,500 setup)

Even with the cheapest offshore VA at $2K/month, you save $17,200 in Year 1. The setup pays for itself in less than a month.

Scenario 2: Saving your own time

If you're doing the work yourself and your time is worth $100/hour:

  • Hours saved per day: 2-4 hours (conservative)
  • Monthly time saved: 60-120 hours
  • Monthly value: $6,000-12,000
  • Year 1 net value: $68,700-140,700

At just 2 hours saved per day, you're looking at 40x return on your Year 1 investment.

Scenario 3: Revenue generation

Your agent doesn't just save time — it can generate revenue.

  • Automated lead outreach: More prospects contacted = more pipeline
  • Faster follow-ups: Fewer leads falling through cracks
  • Overnight code builds: Ship features faster = grow product faster
  • Content creation: More content = more organic traffic = more leads

My own agent helped generate a $48K pipeline through automated outreach. One workflow. One time.

The comparison

OpenClaw (Year 1)

  • Setup: $1,500
  • Monthly: $150
  • Annual total: $3,300
  • Availability: 24/7/365
  • Training time: 0
  • Turnover risk: None

Virtual Assistant (Year 1)

  • Salary: $24,000-60,000
  • Management: ~500 hours of your time
  • Annual total: $24,000-60,000+
  • Availability: 8hrs/day, 5 days/week
  • Training: 2-4 weeks
  • Turnover: Every 6-12 months

When OpenClaw is NOT worth it

  • Very small operations: If you're doing under $5K/month revenue, try DIY first.
  • Mostly human-interaction work: If 90% of the job is phone calls and relationships, an agent won't replace that.
  • No repetitive workflows: If every day is genuinely different, there's less to automate.

For most businesses doing $10K+/month with repetitive operational tasks, the ROI is clear and fast.

Calculate your specific ROI

  1. List your repetitive tasks. How many hours per week?
  2. Value your time. Revenue ÷ hours worked.
  3. Calculate monthly value. Hours saved × hourly value × 4 weeks
  4. Subtract costs. Monthly value - $150 ongoing - ($1,500 ÷ 12 amortized)
  5. That's your monthly ROI.

For most founders I talk to, the number is $3,000-10,000/month in value against $275/month amortized Year 1 cost.
