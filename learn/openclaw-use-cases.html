10 Things My OpenClaw Agent
Does Every Day

Not hypothetical. Not theoretical. These are real tasks my AI agent Jars handles every single day. Here's what that actually looks like.

My agent is called Jars. It runs on OpenClaw, powered by Claude. I've been using it daily for months. It started as a morning briefing bot. Now it handles roughly half my business operations.

Here are 10 things it does every day. All real. All in production right now.

1. Morning briefings

Every morning at 7am, Jars scans my inbox, CRM, calendar, and social notifications. It compiles everything into a clean summary and sends it to me on Telegram.

I wake up, check my phone, and know exactly what needs my attention today. No inbox scrolling. No context switching. Just a bullet-point briefing from my agent.

Time saved: 30-45 minutes every morning

2. CRM management

When new leads come in, Jars scores them based on engagement signals — website visits, email opens, form submissions. It updates deal stages, logs interactions, and flags anything that needs my attention.

I used to spend an hour every day in HubSpot. Now I check it maybe once a week.

Time saved: 45-60 minutes daily

3. GitHub repository backups

Every night, Jars backs up all my GitHub repositories to a separate storage location. If GitHub goes down or something gets deleted, I've got copies. It runs automatically. I never think about it.

Peace of mind: Priceless. But also takes about 2 minutes of agent time.

4. Research and competitor monitoring

Jars monitors my competitors' websites, social media, and product updates. When something changes — new feature, pricing update, blog post — it flags it for me with a summary.

I also ask it for ad-hoc research. "Find me 10 SaaS companies in the fitness space doing $1-5M ARR." It searches, compiles, and delivers. What would take me 2 hours takes the agent 5 minutes.

Time saved: 1-2 hours when I need research

5. Website builds and deployments

This website you're reading right now? Jars built it. I describe what I want in plain English, and the agent writes the HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Then it deploys to Vercel.

I've shipped entire landing pages overnight. I go to bed, describe what I want, and wake up to a deployed website.

Time saved: Hours per page. Multiply that by dozens of pages.

6. Calorie and fitness tracking

This one surprises people. I tell Jars what I ate — "chicken breast, rice, broccoli" — and it logs the macros. It tracks my daily intake and flags when I'm off target.

No app. No barcode scanning. Just a Telegram message. It's faster than any fitness app I've tried.

Time saved: 10-15 minutes daily (vs. manual logging)

7. Email drafting and triage

Jars categorizes my incoming emails by priority. Important client emails get flagged immediately. Newsletters get summarized. Spam gets ignored. For emails that need a response, it drafts replies for my review.

I don't write emails from scratch anymore. I review and edit drafts. Much faster.

Time saved: 30-45 minutes daily

8. Content creation

Blog posts, social media content, email newsletters. I give Jars a topic and some bullet points, and it produces drafts. Not AI slop — I've configured its writing style to match mine. Short sentences. Direct. No fluff.

I still edit everything. But going from draft to final is way faster than going from blank page to final.

Time saved: 1-2 hours per piece of content

9. Scheduling and calendar management

When someone wants to meet, I don't do the back-and-forth. Jars checks my calendar, suggests available slots, and handles the coordination. It also reminds me about upcoming meetings and preps context (who the person is, what we last discussed).

Time saved: 15-20 minutes daily

10. Overnight code builds

This is the killer feature for me. Before bed, I describe a feature I want built for DMpro. Jars works through the night — writing code, running tests, fixing bugs. I wake up to a pull request with the changes ready for review.

I went from shipping 2-3 features per week to 2-3 per day. That's not an exaggeration.

Time saved: 3-4 hours per feature
