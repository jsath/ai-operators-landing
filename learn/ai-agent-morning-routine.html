1. It Processes My Entire Inbox

Time: 6:00 AM ‚Äî takes about 8 minutes

Overnight, emails pile up. Partnership requests, customer questions, newsletter junk, investor updates, team messages. By 6 AM, there's usually 10-20 new messages.

Jars reads every single one. Here's what it does with each:

  • Urgent/action-needed ‚Äî Flagged red, moved to priority inbox. These are the first things I see.
  • Needs response (not urgent) ‚Äî Draft response written and saved. I review and hit send in seconds.
  • Informational ‚Äî Summarized in 1-2 lines. I skim during coffee.
  • Junk/newsletter ‚Äî Archived or unsubscribed. Gone before I ever see it.

The result? Instead of opening Gmail to 18 unread emails and feeling overwhelmed, I open my Slack to a clean summary. The 3 emails that actually matter are highlighted. Draft responses are already written. The rest is handled.

üí° Time saved: ~35 minutes/day

Email triage alone used to eat 30-45 minutes of my morning. Now it's a 5-minute review of what Jars already organized.

2. It Scores and Prioritizes Leads

Time: 6:10 AM ‚Äî takes about 12 minutes

I run DMpro, a SaaS product. Leads come in from multiple channels ‚Äî website forms, Twitter DMs, email, LinkedIn. Overnight, new ones land.

Jars evaluates each lead based on signals I've taught it:

  • Company size ‚Äî Is this a one-person shop or a 50-person team?
  • Engagement level ‚Äî Did they visit pricing? Download something? Reply to outreach?
  • Buying intent ‚Äî Are they asking "how much" or "what is this?"
  • Fit score ‚Äî Do they match our ideal customer profile?

Each lead gets a score from 1-10 and a recommended action: "reach out today," "nurture," or "not a fit." Hot leads (8+) get bumped to the top of my outreach list.

3. It Updates the CRM

Time: 6:22 AM ‚Äî takes about 10 minutes

CRM hygiene is one of those things every founder knows matters but nobody actually does. Deal stages go stale. Contact info gets outdated. Follow-up tasks pile up.

Jars fixes this every morning:

  • Deal stage updates ‚Äî If a lead replied, the deal moves forward. If they ghosted for 7 days, it gets flagged.
  • Contact enrichment ‚Äî New info from emails or social gets added to contact records.
  • Follow-up scheduling ‚Äî Overdue follow-ups get re-queued. New ones get created based on deal stage.
  • Activity logging ‚Äî Every email, call, or interaction gets logged automatically.

Before Jars, my CRM was always 3-5 days behind reality. Now it's current as of 6:30 AM every single day.

5. It Drafts Personalized Outreach

Time: 6:40 AM ‚Äî takes about 15 minutes

This is where the money is made.

Based on the lead scoring from step 2, Jars identifies who needs outreach today. Then it drafts personalized messages for each one:

  • Hot leads (score 8-10) ‚Äî Direct, specific DMs referencing their company and pain points
  • Warm leads (score 5-7) ‚Äî Value-add messages sharing relevant content or case studies
  • Re-engagement ‚Äî Follow-ups for leads that went quiet, with a new angle

Each message is personalized. Not "Hi {first_name}" personalized. Actually personalized ‚Äî referencing their recent tweets, company news, specific challenges in their industry.

By the time I sit down, there are 8-12 outreach drafts waiting for my review. I tweak a few words, approve, and they get sent at optimal times throughout the day.
